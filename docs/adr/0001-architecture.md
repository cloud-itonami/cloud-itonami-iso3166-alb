# ADR-0001: Architecture — Albania market-entry compliance actor (`marketentry`)

**Status**: accepted
**Date**: 2026-07-21

## Context

`cloud-itonami-iso3166-alb` was published as a `:blueprint` (docs +
`blueprint.edn` only, then a country-level `culture.facts` catalog in a
separate Wave 1 batch) but carried ZERO `src/marketentry` or
`src/statute` content -- its `:public-sector/market-entry-compliance`
domain, declared in `blueprint.edn`, was unimplemented. This ADR closes
that gap, following the pattern established by
`cloud-itonami-iso3166-jpn` (origin) and `cloud-itonami-iso3166-bgr` /
`cloud-itonami-iso3166-aze` (the simpler, no-`goyoukiki` shape this
blueprint also uses -- `blueprint.edn`'s `:required-technologies` does
not list `:ontology`, so this fork skips the `marketentry.goyoukiki`
real-tender-fact bridge JPN carries).

## Decision

Build the full governed-actor architecture for `marketentry`, mirroring
JPN/BGR/AZE's harness verbatim (StateGraph node names, governor hard/
escalate contract, phase 0-3 rollout, `Store` protocol with MemStore +
DatomicStore parity) and researching Albania's own real market-entry
rules from scratch for the country-specific content:

- **Store**: `marketentry.store`, MemStore + DatomicStore, proven parity
  via contract test.
- **Registry**: `marketentry.registry`, pure DRAFT-certificate
  construction via `unsigned-certificate`, jurisdiction-scoped sequence
  numbering (`ALB-DFT-000000`, `ALB-SUB-000000`), plus
  `tax-arrears-de-minimis-threshold-all` / `tax-arrears-exceeds-de-
  minimis?` for the flagship check below.
- **Governor**: `:market-entry-compliance-governor` (family keyword from
  `blueprint.edn`).
- **Entity shape**: `engagement`, sequential draft -> submit on the same
  record. `high-stakes` = `#{:actuation/draft-filing
  :actuation/submit-filing}`.
- **Phase**: 0->3; `:filing/draft` and `:filing/submit` NEVER auto-
  commit at any phase.

### Flagship HARD check: `tax-arrears-exceeds-de-minimis` (genuinely new shape)

Bulgaria's flagship check (`tax-arrears-exceeds-threshold`) recomputes a
COMPUTED formula (1% of the operator's own annual turnover, capped at
BGN 50,000); Azerbaijan's (`unreliable-supplier-listed`) is a plain
BOOLEAN registry-membership read. Researching Albania's own Law
No. 162/2020 "On Public Procurement" (WebFetch-verified directly
against the Public Procurement Agency's own official English
translation, downloaded from app.gov.al's legislation page -- read via
direct PDF-text extraction after this iteration's WebFetch summarizer
tool reported the document as unreadable binary) surfaced a genuinely
different shape again: Neni 76(2) makes unpaid taxes/obligatory
social-security contributions a mandatory disqualification ground
UNLESS one of four exceptions applies, and letter "c" of that paragraph
carves out operators who "have not paid small amounts of taxes or
contributions in social insurance up to the value of ALL 10,000" -- a
FLAT statutory constant, independent of the operator's turnover or any
other engagement field. `tax-arrears-exceeds-de-minimis?` recomputes a
plain comparison against that fixed amount. This is a THIRD distinct
check shape for the iso3166 family: not a computed formula (Bulgaria),
not a boolean registry read (Azerbaijan), but a flat-constant threshold
comparison. This namespace models only the letter-"c" numeric carve-out
-- the other three Neni 76(2) exceptions (ongoing litigation, an
existing payment agreement, late notification) are not represented in
this actor's simplified engagement schema, the same honest-scope-
narrowing discipline sibling actors apply to their own jurisdictions'
fuller legal text.

### Other HARD checks (all unoverridable)

1. **spec-basis** -- never invent a jurisdiction's market-entry
   requirements (`marketentry.facts` G2 catalog: Electronic Procurement
   System, QKB, NUIS for ALB).
2. **evidence-incomplete** -- draft/submit require a full assessment
   checklist on file.
3. **tax-arrears-exceeds-de-minimis** -- see above (FLAGSHIP).
4. **engagement-fee-mismatch** -- recompute `base-fee + monthly-rate ×
   monitoring-months` (ground-truth-recompute discipline).
5. **nuis-unverified** -- conditional on `:requires-nuis?` (NUIS / Numri
   Unik i Identifikimit të Subjektit regime, generated by the National
   Business Center / QKB on business registration).
6. **already-drafted / already-submitted** -- dedicated booleans, never
   a `:status` value.

### `rep-spec-basis`: real but narrow, like Bulgaria's

Neni 76(1) of Ligji Nr. 162/2020 mandates disqualification for economic
operators convicted of specified serious criminal offenses, and its
closing sentence extends this personal exclusion ground to natural
persons with representative/decision-making/controlling powers within
the operator -- real and WebFetch-verified, but (like Bulgaria's ЗОП
Art. 54(2)-(3)) NOT a claim that Albania mandates a resident/domestic
representative for public-tender participation itself. This iteration
separately checked APP's own "Register as a foreign operator" intake
page and found a direct foreign-operator registration window with no
stated local-representative mandate, so no stronger claim is made.

### Deliberately not modeled this iteration

APP's Neni 78 power to exclude an economic operator from public
contracts for 3 months to 3 years (misinformation, contractual default,
repeated withdrawal, bid-rigging) and its own published "Excluded
economic operators" list (`app.gov.al/other/excluded-economic-
operators/`) are real and WebFetch-confirmed to exist, but were left
unimplemented as a governor check in favor of the Neni 76(2)(c)
flagship above -- adding a second boolean registry-membership check on
top would have been reaching for scope rather than depth. Left as a
genuine, verified extension point for a future iteration.

### `statute.facts` (second, orthogonal catalog)

Three Albanian statutes. Unlike Bulgaria/Germany, Albania does not fold
its commercial-entity law into the Civil Code (that pattern was
Azerbaijan's, not Albania's) -- it has its OWN standalone commercial-
companies law, Ligji Nr. 9901, datë 14.4.2008, "Për Tregtarët dhe
Shoqëritë Tregtare" (confirmed genuinely distinct from the Civil Code,
Law No. 7850/1994, by direct research rather than assumed by analogy),
cited here from the tax authority's own official download
(`tatime.gov.al`). The Labour Code (Ligji Nr. 7961, datë 12.7.1995) is
cited directly from Albania's official gazette document store
(`qbz.gov.al`), confirmed working by downloading and extracting its
actual PDF text. Personal data protection is cited as Ligji Nr.
124/2024 "Për Mbrojtjen e të Dhënave Personale" (in force 31.01.2025,
REPEALING the older Ligji Nr. 9887/2008 this iteration's initial
secondary-source search surfaced first) -- catching that supersession
required specifically checking for a more recent replacement rather
than trusting the first law number found, cited from the Commissioner
for the Right to Information and Protection of Personal Data's own site
(`idp.al`).

## Consequences

- `src/` now genuinely exists with real, tested, WebFetch-cited content
  for this blueprint's declared domain (`:public-sector/market-entry-
  compliance`) -- moves this repo's `manifest/itonami-fleet-audit.edn`
  `:prod-ready?` signal from `:stub` to `:active`.
- The existing `culture.facts` catalog (Wave 1, unrelated batch) is
  untouched.
- Sibling country blueprints can continue forking JPN/BGR/AZE/ALB and
  swapping in their own genuinely-researched `marketentry.facts` /
  `statute.facts` content and whichever flagship check their own law
  actually supports -- this ADR is itself evidence that the flagship
  check should be chosen from real research, not copied by rote.
